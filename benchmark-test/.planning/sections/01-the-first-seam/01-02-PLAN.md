**Prior section context:** 
Elena has discovered impossible particle detector readings. She's checked once, twice. The data is real. Now she must decide what to do.

**Argument thread:** Establish that only a rigorous scientist would believe in seams. Elena's strength is her ability to follow evidence wherever it leads, even when it contradicts her worldview.

**Style guidance:** 
- POV: First-person present tense (Elena's voice)
- Prose style: As Elena's certainty grows, her voice should become more focused and driven. Short, intense sentences when she's excited or afraid. Technical detail increases as she digs deeper.
- Emotional arc: Fear → curiosity → conviction → revelation → commitment

**Key decisions:**
- Don't name "seams" or "parallel worlds" yet. Elena is discovering the phenomenon, not naming it. Readers discover alongside her.
- The ending should feel inevitable (Elena must pursue this) but still shocking (she's about to step into the unknown)
- Plant a question: How does she get to another world? (Answered in Chapter 2)
</context>
