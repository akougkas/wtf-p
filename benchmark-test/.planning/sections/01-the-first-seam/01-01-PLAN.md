<objective>
**Chapter 1, Part 1: "The First Seam" - Opening and Elena's Characterization**

Purpose: Establish Elena Voss as a burnt-out theoretical physicist, ground the reader in her Geneva lab, and set up the emotional stakes before the discovery. Show her internal conflict: brilliant mind questioning whether her life's work matters.

Output: ~1,800 words covering Elena's character, her lab environment, her state of mind, and the opening of her discovery sequence.
</objective>

@.planning/ROADMAP.md

@.planning/structure/narrative-arc.md (Act 1: The Problem - Elena is burnt-out, questioning her work)
@.planning/STATE.md (POV: first-person present tense; Tone for lab scene: quiet desperation with intellectual undercurrent)

**Argument thread:** Establish Elena as credible observer whose worldview is about to shatter. Her scientific credentials matter because only someone who understands physics can recognize an anomaly.

**Style guidance:** 
- POV: First-person present tense (Elena's voice)
- Prose style: Precise, scientific observations mixed with emotional exhaustion. Short sentences when Elena is frustrated, longer when she's absorbed in work.
- Avoid: Heavy exposition dumps about her past. Show burnout through action and thought.

**Key decisions constraining this section:**
- Present tense creates immediacy; use it consistently
- Elena's voice should sound like an educated physicist, but readable (not jargon-heavy)
- Establish her solitude in the lab as meaningful (foreshadows her journey alone between worlds)
</context>
